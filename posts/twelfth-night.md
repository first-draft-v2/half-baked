There lies just off the coast of Greece
A town called Ilrya
Where life was calm and effortless
’Till rats disturbed the peace

They ate the food and brought disease
That made the townsmen sick
And Violet begged the rats to leave
But they ignored her pleas

As time went by and hunger grew
She tried to find a job
But had no luck and thought she’d ask
The captain and his crew.

“Oh captain won't you help me please
I'm on the verge of tears
I need a job and you could use
My skill and expertise”

The captain laughed a laugh so loud
It echoed through the streets
"I can't accept a girl on board
It's simply not allowed"

She fought her tears and dragged her feet:
She’d lost all trace of hope,
But thought she’d ask the piper
Who was just across the street

“Oh piper won't you help me please,
I’ve nowhere else to go.
I need a job and you could use
My skill and expertise”

The piper smiled a sorry smile,
And shook his filthy head
“You are not suited for the job,
For girls it’s much too vile.
Besides, you'd need to learn a lot,
And that could take a while"

She left the piper in a huff
And cursed his narrow mind:
“I’ll change into a boy and then
I’ll prove I’m tough enough”

She went into a clothing shop
And picked a pair of pants
Then piled her hair onto her head
And placed a hat on top

Then overcome by sudden joy
She went back to the dock
To ask the captain for a job,
And ask him as a _boy_

"Oh captain won't you help me please
I’m on the verge of tears
I need a job and you could use
My skill and expertise'

The captain stroked his tufty beard
And wrinkled up his brow
“I'll help you out, young lad, in fact
I'm glad you volunteered”

They took his vessel out to sea
To catch a pile of fish
“And maybe if we're quick”, he said,
“We'll be in time for tea”.

They tossed the anchor and the bait
Into the open sea
But didn't catch a thing all day,
So all they did was wait.

Then suddenly the line went taut
And violet sat up straight
She reeled the line with all her might
To see what she had caught

Before her stood a massive whale
At least six times her size
The whale let out a wail of pain
And tossed around his tail

When finally the whale calmed down
It swam to Violet's boat
“You know, boy, that was rather rude”
He told her with a frown

She spent the day collecting cats
From every part of town
And asked them if perhaps they could
Get rid of all the rats
